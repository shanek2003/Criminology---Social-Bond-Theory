Social Control Theory - Travis Hirschi (1969)

To start off: This is a control theory which examines why people DON’T commit crime. It is under the assumption that all people are law violators, but there is something that makes one refrain from committing crime.

According to Hirschi, criminal/deviant behavior occur when an individual’s bonds to society are weak or broken. Strong ties to society, such as school, work, and family lead to societal conformity. Social bonds are broken into four elements: Attachment, Commitment, Involvement, and Belief. Individuals’ likelihood of participation in deviant/criminal behavior increases when one of these bonds are broken or weakened.

Attachment - According to Hirschi, when individuals have close ties with school, family, friends, and community they are more likly to have respect towards themselves and their attachments. They do not want to ruin relationships by being deviant.

Commitment - When people participate in conventional activities, such as school, work, and goals for future success, they may not want to jeopardize their future. They are commited to their goals, work, school, etc.

Involvement - Commitment to activities such as sports, extracarricular activities, school and work takes up a lot of time. Involvement in these activties leave little time for any criminal activties. When one has nothing to do, on the other hand, they have time to partcipate in deviant/criminal acts. Conventional activites isolate people from doing the wrong thing.

Belief - Holding strong moral values, religious beliefs instill a commitment to believing in societal rules and norms. Individuals who hold strong morals and religious beliefs are more likely to conform to societal rules and less likely to engage in deviant/criminal behavior.

Beliefs are taught by ATTACHMENTS, reinforced by COMMITMENTS and INVOLVEMENTS
